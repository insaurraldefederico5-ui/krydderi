Design principles (from B2B foodservice best practice)

The research is consistent: modern B2B buyers (now mostly millennials/Gen-Z) expect a B2C-grade experience but with B2B rules — account pricing, reorder, net terms, order history without phoning a rep. The portals that win make reordering a single action, surface account-specific pricing and real stock, and work on mobile 24/7. Build to that standard.

Concretely:


Reorder is the hero action, not "browse catalog." Put "Reorder last order" and "My usuals" at the top of the logged-in home.
Density with calm. Pros want a lot of info (price, pack, lead time, cert) but not clutter. Use whitespace and clear hierarchy.
Never make them hunt for price or availability. If those two questions aren't answered instantly, people fall back to email — defeating the portal.
Speed is a feature. Server-render, lazy-load images, keep the cart instant.

Hero page content brief (Phase 2)

Sections, top to bottom:


Nav bar — logo, "Sortiment/Range," "Om os/About," "Kontakt," and a prominent Log ind button + secondary Anmod om konto / Request account.
Hero — headline + subhead + two CTAs. Suggested headline (Danish): "Krydderier direkte fra oprindelsen. Leveret i København." (Spices direct from origin. Delivered in Copenhagen.) Subhead: same certified quality as your current supplier, better price, full traceability, 48-hour delivery. Background: warm, premium spice imagery (whole cardamom, turmeric).
Value props (3 cards) — Origin-direct (cut the German/Dutch middleman), Certified & traceable (organic, Fairtrade, CoA with every batch), Reliable Copenhagen logistics (48h delivery, one supplier for your whole spice list).
How it works (3 steps) — Request an account → We set up your pricing → Order & reorder online anytime.
Range preview — category tiles (Cardamom, Turmeric, Ginger, Herbs, Blends) — visual, no prices (prices are post-login).
Trust strip — certification logos (EU Organic, Fairtrade), "lab-tested every shipment," origin map (Guatemala, India, Turkey, Spain).
Who it's for — bakeries, restaurants, manufacturers, health-food brands.
Lead-capture CTA — "Request an account" form (business name, contact, email, phone, segment, what they currently buy/spend). Submitting writes to leads and emails me.
Footer — company info, CVR, contact, Danish food-business registration note, privacy (GDPR).


Keep it honest and calm — the buyer is a procurement professional, not an impulse shopper.

Microcopy & states to not forget


Empty states: "You haven't ordered yet — browse the range or request your usuals list from us."
Below-minimum-order message: "Add X kr. more to reach your 800 kr. minimum order."
Out-of-stock / lead-time: show "Ships in 3–4 weeks (direct import)" vs "In stock, delivers in 48h."
Order status pills with clear colors.
Price-paid reassurance on product page: "Your price: 195,00 kr./kg" + small "stable since March" if unchanged.
Confirmation email: order summary, ex/MOMS/inc, requested delivery, "reply to this email or use chat for changes."
